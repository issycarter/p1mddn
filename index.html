Intro

About

Space is an expansive place that holds an intricate communication network. The discovery of new information from deep space is due to the successful deep space communication network. This pitch covers the communication network that occurs when reaching robots, and space crafts on missions, and how we receive information back on earth. Most deep space missions never come back to earth which is why it is important to have a strong communication network when learning about what is out there. The demand for deep space communication channels are continuing to increase as more robots go deeper into space. For example. this image of a black hole was received via deep space communication.

Aim

The goal for my platform is to create and interactive website that provides live a live feed /stream of up to date information space. This also includes an interactive map / scenario of how long it has taken to send and receive a message from the different robots in space. It will show how far the different robots are placed and how long it is taking to receive/ send a message. The map will allow speed up of time and zooming in and out the different places of the space map. This is to make users understand the importance of space exploration and the driving forces behind science. Space travel allow humans learn more about why the galaxies came to be, and why it is our duty to discover as much as possible for the future.

Components

User profiles

The goal for my project is to reach a wide age range of people. The idea of space networks appears as an intimidating subject to many audiences. It can often appear to use scientific jargon and be very technical. I aim to break down this stigma behind space network perception an to spark the interest of school & university students, Millennials, and Boomers. I aim to capture these age group through providing simple and understandable explanations about the processes, as well as providing answers to general questions about space travel. Using bold imagery and colours will assist with grabbing the user’s attention.

Inspiration 1

(NASA, 2020)

This is my first reference. NASA’s website resource is a live high definition viewing system of earth and of satellites. The website breaks down how it records the information, the goals of creating a live stream, and the history of who got involved with this project. The depth of understandable information on this page is something I want replicate on my project.

Inspiration 2

(Radio Garden, 2020)

Radio Garden is an interactive globe interface where you can click on different regions and view the different radio station. I have taken inspiration from the interactivity of the different regions on the globe. For my project I want to be able to zoom out of space to be able to see how far the different robots and satellites are located which are interactable like Radio Garden.

Inspiration 3

(Google Earth, 2020)

Google earth is another reference point. The interface of the map has smooth transitions when zooming out. Zooming in provides a lot of information of continents however I want to create the same effect when zooming out of the solar system.

Inspiration 4

(Spacejam, 2020)

My website aesthetic takes inspiration from Space Jam. The layout is playful and approachable, creating an fun interface to interact with. Each planet/link circles the center much like space and is very effective when creating a focal point on the page.
